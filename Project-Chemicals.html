Modernising software for chemical labs

Problem

In the world of laboratory equipment, things are complex: there are numerous different tools with their own intricate workflows and UIs; new equipment is constantly being developed, and labs frequently change their setups. Users range from occasional novices to hardcore power users. Consequently, a major hardware producer decided to leverage their experience and build a common software platform, which could be offered to their customers as a paid software solution.

Solution

Our team was building a multi-platform web application designed to support a plug-in approach for integrating additional equipment, while also providing basic common functionality as a default. My contributions to facilitate this included: a design system with theming capabilities, a library of flexible layouts, and comprehensive documentation.

Results

The platform's development is ongoing, though I am no longer part of the project. My work enabled the team to continue uninterrupted development without a dedicated designer for a period.

Learnings

  • The more complex the domain, the more crucial it is to engage with actual users through interviews and prototype testing.
  • Similarly, user personas are essential for such complex functionality.
  • Working on projects within regulated environments introduces unexpected constraints.
  • Accessibility must be a top priority.
  • The stakeholders' decision not to specifically cater to touch devices created complications later in the development process.
  • Labeling something an MVP while expecting a full-fledged final application does not magically expedite its development within an MVP timeline.
