Car buying platform

Problem

A prominent European automotive business determined that their current car buying division was not generating the expected revenue. This shortfall was attributed to the system's outdated technology, poor UX and deprecated features and workflows.

Solution

Having previously established a common platform, this project involved integrating the new car buying business into it. My primary responsibilities included designing user flows tailored to each team involved in the car buying process, ensuring their specific needs were met and creating an efficient and user-friendly work environment.

Results

While the project is still in pre-launch testing, preliminary internal testing results are promising. During several user testing sessions we received positive feedback and the business anticipates a 6-7% increase in processed leads within the first quarter following launch.

Learnings

  • Leveraging the existing common platform and design system enabled us to completely redesign a complex system within a surprisingly short timeframe.
  • Investing significant time in developing detailed user flows and thoroughly reviewing them with stakeholders proved more effective than proceeding directly to wireframes after initial internal and PO discussions.
  • User interviews were crucial in identifying and addressing any missing requirements resulting from the project's complexity and the accelerated initial requirements gathering phase.
